 Brill 1964 , Brown et al. 1977      The levels given with three places after the decimal point are quoted from interferometric measurements of Brill 1964 . They are accurate to ±0.006 cm-1 on average, although a few have uncertainties up to ±0.021 cm-1. For the levels given with two places after the decimal point, the average uncertainty is ±0.07 cm-1. Levels given with one place after the decimal point are estimated to be accurate to ±2.5 cm-1. They are based on the measurements of Meggers 1940 . Autoionizing levels given as integer numbers have an average uncertainty of ±8 cm-1, except for the broad 5s5p3 1,3P° levels, which are accurate to ±80 cm-1. The most accurate published level values have been selected from Brill 1964 , Brown et al. 1977 , and from Forrest et al. 1985 . Configuration and term assignments and percentage compositions of the even parity levels are from Oliver and Hibbert 2008 ; those for the odd parity levels are due to Dembczyński and Rebel 1984  and to Dembczynski and Wilson 1988 . The Landé factors given with uncertainties are quoted from the measurements of Xu et al. 2009 . The rest of the Landé factors are from Moore 1958 . The ionization limits are from Brown et al. 1977 .
