 Sugar&Musgrove 1990 , Shenstone 1948 , Civiš et al. 2012 , Tondello 1973 , Longmire et al. 1980      The data given here are based on the energy level list compiled by Sugar&Musgrove 1990 . This list was extended with [3d10]ng, nh, and 7f levels found from infrared Fourier-transform spectra by Civiš et al. 2012 . Those authors also observed transitions involing levels of other [3d10]nl configurations with n ≥ 5 and l = s, p, d, f, which resulted in significant refinement of these levels. The level values given here are determined from observed wavelengths using the least-squares optimization code LOPT (Kramida 2011 ). Positions of most of the 3d10nl levels above 43 000 cm-1 relative to 3d105p 2P°3/2 (at 49382.945(17) cm-1) are now known with uncertainties ranging from 0.003 to 0.015 cm-1. However, the connection of these excited levels to the ground state is based entirely on the grating measurements of Shenstone 1948 in the near ultraviolet, visible, and near infrared ranges and has a significant uncertainty of ±0.017 cm-1. Re-measurement of the spectrum in these ranges using Fourier transform spectroscopy could greatly improve the accuracy. The odd-parity levels above the first ionization limit are based entirely on the absorption measurements of Tondello 1973 in the far-ultraviolet region. These measurements had an uncertainty of about 0.02 Å for most of the lines. This absorption spectrum was re-analyzed by Baig et al. 1997 with a much better resolution. We used only one line from their work, 1202.753(6) Å, to determine the 3d94s(3D3)10f level, which was not observed by Tondello. Assessment of the other results of Baig et al. 1997 requires an extensive theoretical analysis of the structure of the 3d94snl configurations, which has not been done so far. The present data set was prepared by A. Kramida and L. I. Podobedova (2014, unpublished). Literature on Cu I Energy Levels
